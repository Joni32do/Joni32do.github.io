## Analysis
Four semesters

### Basics

#### I. Foundations

* Propositional logic, sets, (equivalent) relations,
* mappings, Peano, real numbers (algebra, order, topo, Eudoxus)
* (Cauchy) sequences & limits, infinity, complex numbers, FTA

#### II. Metric and Topology

* Convergence, metric space, Supremum
* limits (police, Bolzano, Cantor, Weierstrass) e.g. Euler 
* Euclidean spaces, open & closed, limits of functions
* (uniform) Continuity, space C, exp, compact sets,

#### III. Derivatives

* Properties, tricks, examples, Landau
* Theorem (Rolle, Fermat, Cauchy, Lagrange), FTD
* Higher order, Taylor, monoton, extrema
* Convexity, L'hopital, Darboux, roots

#### IV. Integration

* Riemann-Integral, structure, properties, monoton
* Newton-Leibniz formula, Substitution of variables
* Mean value theorem, Taylor remainder
* Arc length, polynomial long division, volumes
* Numeric integration, Simpson, Trapez

#### V. Series and improper Integrals

* Cauchy-Criteria (linearity), not neg. sum
* Riemann series theorem, convergence criteria (limes)
* Criteria Abel, Dirichlet, Kummerer, Leibniz, Caesaro, Frobenius, Hardy
* Infinit products, sum of divergent series

#### VI. Sequences and Series of Functions

* Uniformity, direct comparison test
* Limit swap, limit & integral swap
* diff. & int. of param. integrals, cont. & diff. of integrals with param. limits
* Power series, radius of convergence

#### VII. Derivatives of multivariable functions

* Equivalence of norms, linear & cont. operators
* (Frechet, Gateaux , partial) derivative, FTD
* Hahn-Banach, mappings in $\mathbb{R}^n$
* Higher order derivatives, Taylor, extrema, FTA
* Weierstrass & Stone
